Science & AI

Teaching Machines to See

I hold a PhD in AI and Earth Observation from Politehnica University of Bucharest. I taught machines to read satellite images — and to resist attacks that try to fool them.

Satellites photograph the Earth every day, creating enormous amounts of data. AI can interpret these images, but it can be tricked. My research developed defense strategies — active learning, physics-aware models, and synthetic image generation — to make these systems smarter and harder to deceive.

About

What I Care About

I care about people caught between systems — between countries, between old rules and new technologies, between what they're allowed to say and what they need to express.

I was born in Iran. I studied engineering, then moved to Europe for a PhD in artificial intelligence. Along the way, I never stopped watching people — how they survive, how they create, how they keep their dignity.

I studied documentary filmmaking at Shahid Saless Film School in Tehran. I've spoken at Iranian cultural events in Bucharest and Timișoara. I use science, cinema, AI, and music to build small spaces of freedom — for myself, for my friends back home, and for anyone who recognizes this in-between place.
